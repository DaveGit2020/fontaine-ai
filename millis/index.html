STUDENT SAFETY & SCHOOL OPERATIONS
Your Students Come First. Always.
Every phase of this project is designed around one non-negotiable: students are safe, school runs normally, and the community is never disrupted. This isn't a section of our plan — it's the foundation of everything we do.

Bus & Drop-Off Protection

Segregated circulation paths. "Blackout hours" — zero construction deliveries during student arrival and dismissal. High-visibility crosswalks with strobe lights, proven at East Longmeadow HS.

Air Quality Containment

Negative air machines, temporary sealed barriers, dust monitoring. Our IAQ containment protocols are so effective we've been recruited by active healthcare environments. That's the standard we bring to Millis.

Noise & Recreation

Quiet hours during testing. Loud work scheduled during low-impact windows. Outdoor recreation space maintained every single phase — documented in our site logistics plan. Student life doesn't stop for construction.

Construction Traffic

Construction staff parking completely separated from school community. Truck queuing managed off surrounding streets. Police coordination for high-impact deliveries.

Security & Access

Construction areas fully fenced and locked. All workers badged and background-checked. Separate entrances maintained throughout all phases. Principal updated weekly.

Emergency Planning

Fire department walkthroughs before each phase. Emergency egress routes never compromised. Evacuation plans updated and drilled with school administration per phase transition.

Phase 1 — New Middle School Addition & Gymnasium (Mar 2027 – Jul 2028)

Construction of the new middle school wing and gymnasium addition. Sitework and foundations begin March 2027. Steel erection May through July 2027. Building envelope tight by January 2028. Interior fitout and commissioning through spring. Substantial completion July 14, 2028 — ready for students to occupy when school opens. This is the anchor phase that enables every subsequent renovation.

Phase 2 — Locker Rooms & Boiler Room (Summer Slammer #1)

10-week concentrated renovation window. The boiler room is the mechanical heart of the building — all new systems in the Phase 1 addition must route back to this renovated core. Scope includes hazmat abatement, selective demolition, underslab work, roofing, and complete interior fitout. ERU equipment procured early and stored on site: "We want to be looking AT it — not looking FOR it." Every inspection mapped backward from first day of school.

Phase 3 — Gym, Admin & Cafeteria (Summer Slammer #2)

The most operationally sensitive summer of the project. Students transition into the completed addition while we renovate the existing gym, administrative offices, and cafeteria. This phase requires fabrication-level VDC coordination, MEP shutdown rehearsals, and pre-renovation LiDAR scanning to verify every existing condition. IAQ containment protocols at healthcare-grade standards. Early commissioning and phased punchlist ensure the addition is fully operational before students arrive.

Phase 4 — Classroom Wing Renovation (Jun 2028 – Feb 2029)

Largest renovation concentration in the project. Critical decisions: 1960s CMU walls requiring structural reinforcement — fire ratings conflict with new MEP routing through the same walls, requiring virtual modeling before any demolition. Solar panel scope must be resolved early: Who owns them? How old are they? Does performance meet future design? What are the warranty terms for removal and reinstallation? Roof replacement strategy — phase by phase or one comprehensive replacement as the final step? These are precon conversations with Tappe and Vertex in the first weeks.

Phase 5 — Demo Wing & High School Entry (Feb – Aug 2029)

Hazmat abatement (30 days), selective building demolition (30 days), followed by construction of the new high school main entry. Site logistics shift significantly as the demo area opens up new staging and access opportunities. Careful coordination with ongoing school operations during the spring semester.

Phase 5A — Cafeteria, Theater & Music (Summer Slammer #3)

Final renovation push. Cafeteria expansion, theater improvements, and music room renovation. The project transitions from active construction to final operations handoff. Every system commissioned, every space inspected, every piece of equipment verified. Final completion: August 31, 2029. Our goal is perfect operations from day one — not just an on-time handoff.

MILLIS-SPECIFIC CHALLENGES
Challenges We've Already Identified & Solved For.
These aren't generic risks from a template. These are Millis-specific challenges our team identified during our proposal preparation — and exactly how we'll address each one.
STRUCTURAL

1960s CMU Walls

The original 1960s concrete masonry walls need structural reinforcement. Fire ratings conflict with new MEP routing through the same walls. Our approach: model it virtually first using LiDAR point cloud data, resolve every conflict before we touch a single block.

MEP CRITICAL

Boiler Room Routing

The boiler room is the mechanical heart of this building. New systems in the Phase 1 addition must chase back to the renovated boiler room core. We confirm all routing paths during preconstruction — renovating from the core out, not the other way around.

PROCUREMENT

ERU Equipment Timing

"We want to be looking AT it — not looking FOR it." Energy recovery units and long-lead equipment are bought early, stored on site, and ready to install before the summer window opens. No scrambling for equipment during a 10-week crunch.

EXISTING CONDITIONS

Solar Panel Scope

Five questions that must be answered early: Who owns them? How old are they? Does current performance meet future design requirements? What are the warranty terms for removal and reinstallation? Is this work on the critical path? These conversations happen in the first weeks of preconstruction.

ENVELOPE

Roof Replacement Strategy

How much existing roof needs to be replaced? Do we replace per-phase as we go, or install one comprehensive new roof as the final step? Each approach has different schedule, cost, and warranty implications. This is an early precon decision with Tappe and Vertex.

AIR QUALITY

IAQ During Occupied Renovation

Negative air machines, sealed temporary barriers, continuous dust monitoring. Our IAQ containment protocols were developed for occupied schools and have been so effective we've been recruited into active healthcare environments. That's the standard we bring to every renovation phase at Millis.

SCHEDULE

Summer Commissioning Windows

Each summer phase has unique inspection and commissioning requirements that must be complete before students return. All testing, balancing, and inspections are mapped backward from the first day of school — not crammed into the last week of August.

OPERATIONS

School Space Clearing

Summers are too short to lose days boxing and moving stored items. We build space clearing commitments into the planning calendar months in advance, working with school administration to ensure every room is ready when the renovation window opens.

TECHNOLOGY & BIM
Renovation BIM Is Fundamentally Different.
In new construction, the BIM model IS the building. In renovation, the gaps are in what's NOT in the model — underslab conditions, existing foundations, legacy piping without correct elevations. Our approach: proactively model what others leave out.

In-House LiDAR Scanning

Millions of data points capturing real existing conditions — architectural, structural, and MEP. Not assumptions from 1960s drawings. Verified field data integrated into every coordination model before any summer work begins.

Case Studies — We Model What Others Leave Out

SOUTH HIGH COMMUNITY SCHOOL

Underslab Drainage

Challenge: Design included underslab drainage not normally shown in coordination models. The structural model was missing foundations entirely.

Our solution: Fontaine modeled ALL foundations from the structural drawings, then drew the underslab drainage, rerouting piping around foundations where conflicts existed.

Result: Zero conflicts during installation. Dimensioned drawings produced and issued directly to the site contractor.

SOUTH HIGH COMMUNITY SCHOOL

Storm Drain Beam Penetration

Challenge: Roof drains traveling 150 feet required a penetration through a first-floor structural beam. The design model showed the pipe with no pitch — physically impossible.

Our solution: Modeled the correct pitch, identified the beam penetration need, and flagged it before steel was fabricated.

Result: Structural engineer adjusted the design. Zero rework. Zero change orders.

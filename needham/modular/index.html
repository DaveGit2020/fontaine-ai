THE CHALLENGE
Why Modulars Are Required
Building a new school on the existing Pollard campus means the current building stays occupied through Phase 1 construction. As Phase 2 requires demolition of the existing school, temporary classroom space must be planned, procured, installed, and operational before students vacate the old building. This isn't an afterthought — it's a critical-path item.

PROCUREMENT OPTIONS
Three Paths. One Decision.
Each option carries different cost, schedule, and quality tradeoffs. Our recommendation accounts for Pollard's tight site, the 2-story requirement, and the need for a reliable, code-compliant learning environment for 3+ years.
OPTION 1

Relocate Existing + Supplement

Move available used modulars to the Pollard site and supplement with additional pre-owned units to meet the full classroom count.

ESTIMATED MODULAR COST
~$4M
Minimum — modulars only, before infrastructure
ADVANTAGES
Lowest base cost of all three options
Faster procurement — units already exist
Can leverage district-owned or state surplus inventory
CONSIDERATIONS
Condition varies — inspection and refurb costs
May not meet current code without modifications
Availability not guaranteed at time of need
OPTION 2

Acquire Pre-Owned Units

Purchase pre-owned modular classrooms from the secondary market and have them transported, refurbished, and installed on the Pollard site.

ESTIMATED MODULAR COST
~$5–6M
Mid-range — depends on availability and condition
ADVANTAGES
Broader selection from national market
Can be refurbished to near-new condition
Known inventory — can inspect before committing
CONSIDERATIONS
Transportation costs from distant locations
Refurbishment timeline adds lead time
2-story configuration may limit availability
OPTION 3

Procure New Units

Order brand-new modular classroom buildings manufactured to specification, including purpose-built 2-story structures with integrated stairs and elevator.

ESTIMATED MODULAR COST
~$8M+
Cost doubles for new 2-story configuration
ADVANTAGES
Full code compliance from day one
Custom layout optimized for Pollard site
Best learning environment — HVAC, acoustics, lighting
CONSIDERATIONS
Highest cost — approximately double Option 1
Longest lead time for manufacturing
Temporary asset — significant spend for ~3 year use
BEYOND THE MODULARS
Infrastructure Is the Real Cost Driver.
The modular units themselves are only part of the equation. Pollard's site requires significant infrastructure to support a 2-story temporary campus — and these costs apply regardless of which procurement option is selected.

Key Questions for the Selection Committee

1

2
Are there existing district-owned modulars that could be relocated and supplemented?
3
What is the preferred siting location on the Pollard campus? Our site logistics plan has a recommended location, but Town input is critical.
4
Should the modular cost be included in the GMP or treated as a separate owner-direct procurement?
